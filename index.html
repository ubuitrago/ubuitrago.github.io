About Me

I grew up in San Antonio, Texas as the son of first-generation immigrants- my Dad in the Air Force, my Mom a hospice aide—where I learned early that hard work makes the "American Dream" possible. A STEM camp in high school introduced me to programming and set me on the path to engineering. I earned my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from UT Austin in 2020, but quickly realized my passion was in tech: it moved faster, was more accessible, and anyone could build the next killer app with open-source tools. Today, I focus on software engineering, security, and DevOps—building things that make a difference. I am also wrapping up my Master's of Science in Software Engineering from UT Austin in May 2026.

Interests

From my professional work and studies, I have been exposed to many interesting areas of Computer Science and Software Engineering. When it comes to Cyber Security, I have spent several years researching and developing Zero-Trust solutions. Specifically, I have domain knowledge hardening containers via custom policy generation and debloating. Because of this, I've also learned about container escape strategies and kernel vulnerabilities.

Full-stack engineering is also a lot fun. I have built web-apps using frameworks like React and Flask. My favorite ASGI is FastAPI, as it can handle thousands of requests concurrently; using threads efficiently. Typically, Python is my language of choice, especially when it comes to Machine Learning applications. Training classifiers is very accessible these days, and combining them with LLMs via frameworks like LangChain can support many types of AI applications. Checkout my project DroneZero, where I augment Drone Control with AI.

Below are some general topics I gravitate towards:

  • Drone (UAVs) Technologies and Aircraft
  • Container hardening and debloating
  • Aesthetic React sites & web-apps
  • LLM tools and frameworks
